Accessibility statement

The Department for Education is committed to making its services accessible, in accordance with the Public Sector Bodies (Websites and Mobile Applications) (No. 2) Accessibility Regulations 2018.

This accessibility statement applies to the Calculate Funding Service or CFS website under the aegis of the Education Skills Funding Agency or ESFA of the Department for Education.

We are in the process of conducting a full accessibility audit and will be updating our statement soon. However, making the service fully compliant may not be feasible due to the small number of users currently using the website.

At the moment we are striving to ensure that the CFS website is at a minimum accessible capacity for users with disabilities most commonly found in the workforce.

Compliance status

This website is deemed to be partially compliant with the Web Content Accessibility guidelines version 2.1 AA standard, due to potential non-compliances and exemptions which may be listed below once a full accessibility audit is conducted.

We are striving to ensure that the users of the CFS website are able to:

  • change colours, contrast levels and fonts
  • zoom in up to 300% without the text spilling off the screen
  • navigate most of the service website using just a keyboard
  • navigate most of the service website using speech recognition software
  • listen to most of the service website using a screen reader (including the most recent versions of JAWS, NVDA and VoiceOver)

We've also made the service text on the website as simple as possible to understand.

AbilityNet (opens in a new tab) has advice on making your device easier to use if you have a disability.

Non-accessible content

This section will be updated once we conduct a full accessibility audit.

What we're doing to improve accessibility

We will enhance our service in accordance with the findings of our accessibility audit that is to be conducted soon.

Feedback and contact information

If you need information on this service in a different format like an accessible PDF, large print, easy read, audio recording or braille, email esfa.govuk-enquiries@education.gov.uk.

Reporting accessibility problems with this service

We're always looking to improve the accessibility of this website. If you find any problems not listed on this page or think we're not meeting accessibility requirements, email esfa.govuk-enquiries@education.gov.uk.

Enforcement procedure

The Equality and Human Rights Commission (EHRC) is responsible for enforcing the Public Sector Bodies (Websites and Mobile Applications) (No. 2) Accessibility Regulations 2018 (the 'accessibility regulations'). If you're not happy with how we respond to your complaint, contact the Equality Advisory and Support Service (opens in a new tab).

Preparation of this accessibility statement

This statement was prepared on 20 October 2023.
